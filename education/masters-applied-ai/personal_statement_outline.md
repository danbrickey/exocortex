4. **Already engaged with AI**
   - Not theoretical interest—you're actively leading AI adoption
   - Learning on your own (shows initiative and self-direction)

5. **Long-term commitment to learning**
   - 27 years in field, still pursuing advanced degree

### Things to Avoid

1. **Don't apologize for 1997 GPA** (unless specifically asked)
   - 28 years of professional success speaks louder
   - Your learning ability is proven through career progression

2. **Don't position as "career change"**
   - This is evolution and acceleration, not pivot
   - AI needs exactly what you've built over 27 years

3. **Don't oversell or undersell**
   - Be confident but not arrogant
   - Acknowledge what you're learning while showcasing expertise
   - "I know data architecture deeply; I'm building AI expertise to complement it"

4. **Don't make it all about you**
   - Show how your success will benefit UVU program (real-world case studies, healthcare expertise to share)
   - Show how you'll contribute to cohort learning
   - Demonstrate commitment to broader healthcare AI impact

5. **Don't use too much jargon**
   - Admissions committee may not all be technical
   - Show sophistication without drowning in acronyms
   - Explain technical concepts clearly

## Writing Process Recommendations

### Step 1: Choose Your Opening
- Read all three options
- Pick the one that feels most authentic to you
- Or blend elements from multiple options

### Step 2: Brain Dump Each Section
- Use bullet points above as guide
- Write without editing—get ideas down
- Include more than you'll use (cut later)

### Step 3: First Draft
- Stitch sections together
- Don't worry about perfection
- Focus on flow and completeness

### Step 4: Edit for Clarity
- Cut jargon, add explanations
- Ensure logical flow between paragraphs
- Check that each paragraph has clear purpose

### Step 5: Edit for Length
- Trim to fit word count
- Every sentence should earn its place
- Prioritize specific examples over general claims

### Step 6: Polish
- Read aloud (catches awkward phrasing)
- Check grammar and spelling
- Ensure consistent tone throughout

### Step 7: Get Feedback
- Share with mentor, colleague, or friend
- Ask: "What stands out? What's unclear? What's missing?"
- Revise based on feedback

### Step 8: Final Review
- Let it sit for 24 hours
- Read with fresh eyes
- Make final tweaks
- Submit!
