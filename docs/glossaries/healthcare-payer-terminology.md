# Healthcare Insurance Payer Terminology Glossary

Common healthcare insurance payer terminology and concepts sorted by frequency of use in data platform and analytics contexts.

## Core Insurance Concepts

**Member**
Individual enrolled in health insurance plan with coverage for medical services and benefits.

**Claim**
Itemized statement from healthcare provider requesting payment for services rendered to member.

**Eligibility**
Member's enrollment status and coverage determination for specific services, dates, and benefit categories.

**Premium**
Monthly payment made by employer or member for insurance coverage regardless of service utilization.

**Provider**
Healthcare professional, facility, or organization delivering medical services to members (physicians, hospitals, clinics).

**Plan**
Specific insurance product with defined benefits, networks, cost-sharing, and coverage rules.

**Group**
Employer or organization purchasing insurance coverage for employees/members under contract with payer.

**Subscriber**
Primary policyholder responsible for premium payments, also called member or enrollee.

**Dependent**
Family member (spouse, child) covered under subscriber's insurance policy.

## Claims Processing

**Adjudication**
Process of reviewing and determining payment for submitted claims based on coverage rules and contracts.

**Paid Amount**
Actual payment made by payer to provider after adjudication, discounts, and cost-sharing calculations.

**Allowed Amount**
Maximum reimbursement payer permits for service based on contracts, fee schedules, or negotiated rates.

**Charged Amount**
Amount submitted on the claimn from the provider.

**Denied Claim**
Claim rejected for payment due to eligibility issues, coverage exclusions, or administrative errors.

**Pended Claim**
Claim held in suspended status requiring additional information or review before adjudication.

**EOB (Explanation of Benefits)**
Statement sent to member explaining claim processing, payment amounts, and member responsibility.

**Remittance Advice**
Payment notification sent to provider detailing claims paid, denied, and adjustment reasons.

**Claim Line**
Individual service or procedure within claim represented by CPT/HCPCS code with associated charges.

**Claim Status**
Current state of claim in processing lifecycle (received, pending, paid, denied, adjusted).

## Financial Terms

**Deductible**
Amount member must pay out-of-pocket before insurance begins paying for covered services annually.

**Copay (Copayment)**
Fixed dollar amount member pays for specific service at time of care (e.g., $20 for office visit).

**Coinsurance**
Percentage of allowed amount member pays after deductible is met (e.g., 20% coinsurance).

**Out-of-Pocket Maximum**
Annual limit on member's cost-sharing after which payer pays 100% of covered services.

**Member Responsibility**
Total amount member owes for services including deductible, copay, and coinsurance.

**COB (Coordination of Benefits)**
Process determining payment order when member has coverage from multiple insurance plans.

**Medical Loss Ratio (MLR)**
Percentage of premium revenue spent on medical care and quality improvement (ACA requires 80-85%).

**PMPM (Per Member Per Month)**
Cost or revenue metric calculated by dividing total amount by member months for normalized comparison.

**Risk Adjustment**
Payment model adjusting reimbursement based on member health status and predicted costs using diagnosis codes and pharmaceutical prescriptions.

## Provider Network & Contracts

**Network**
Group of providers contracted with payer to deliver services at negotiated rates to members.

**In-Network Provider**
Provider with contract and agreement with payer offering lower member cost-sharing.

**Out-of-Network Provider**
Provider without payer contract resulting in higher member costs or no coverage.

**Primary Care Physician (PCP)**
Provider coordinating member's care and providing referrals to specialists in some plan types.

**Specialist**
Provider with expertise in specific medical area often requiring PCP referral depending on plan.

**Fee Schedule**
Standardized payment rates for services based on CPT/HCPCS codes in provider contracts.

**Capitation**
Payment model paying provider fixed amount per member regardless of services rendered (PMPM).

**Fee-for-Service (FFS)**
Payment model reimbursing provider for each service or procedure performed.

**Contracted Rate**
Negotiated payment amount between payer and provider for specific services in network.

## Medical Coding & Classification

**ICD-10 (International Classification of Diseases)**
Diagnosis coding system classifying diseases, symptoms, and health conditions for claims and analytics.

**CPT (Current Procedural Terminology)**
Code set describing medical procedures and services performed by providers for billing.

**HCPCS (Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System)**
Code set for procedures, supplies, and services not included in CPT including DME and drugs.

**DRG (Diagnosis-Related Group)**
Inpatient classification grouping similar diagnoses for hospital payment under Medicare and some plans.

**Modifier**
Two-digit code appended to CPT/HCPCS providing additional information about service circumstances.

**Principal Diagnosis**
Primary reason for healthcare encounter or hospitalization driving treatment and payment.

**HCC (Hierarchical Condition Category)**
Risk adjustment model using diagnosis groupings to predict costs and adjust capitation payments.

## Plan Types & Products

**HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)**
Plan requiring PCP selection, referrals for specialists, and in-network care with lower premiums.

**PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)**
Plan offering flexibility to see any provider with lower costs for in-network care without referrals.

**EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization)**
Plan covering only in-network care without requiring PCP or referrals, no out-of-network benefits.

**HDHP (High Deductible Health Plan)**
Plan with high deductible and low premiums, often paired with HSA for tax-advantaged savings.

**Medicare Advantage (Part C)**
Private insurance alternative to Original Medicare bundling Parts A, B, and often D with additional benefits.

**Medicaid**
Joint federal-state program providing coverage for low-income individuals and families with varying eligibility.

**Commercial Insurance**
Private health insurance purchased by employers or individuals, distinct from government programs.

**Self-Funded Plan**
Employer assumes financial risk for employee healthcare costs, paying claims directly rather than premiums.

## Benefit & Coverage

**Formulary**
List of prescription drugs covered by plan organized in tiers with different cost-sharing levels.

**Prior Authorization**
Pre-approval requirement from payer before provider performs specific services or prescribes medications.

**Medical Necessity**
Criteria requiring services to be appropriate, evidence-based, and essential for diagnosis or treatment.

**Exclusion**
Service or condition explicitly not covered by insurance plan under any circumstances.

**Benefit Period**
Timeframe for which benefits, deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums apply, typically calendar year.

**Lifetime Maximum**
Total amount payer will pay over member's lifetime (prohibited by ACA for essential benefits).

**Carve-Out**
Specific benefit (pharmacy, behavioral health, dental) managed separately by specialized vendor.

## Quality & Compliance

**HEDIS (Healthcare Effectiveness Data and Information Set)**
Standardized performance measures for health plan quality and effectiveness developed by NCQA.

**Star Ratings**
CMS quality measure system for Medicare Advantage and Part D plans (1-5 stars).

**NCQA (National Committee for Quality Assurance)**
Organization accrediting health plans and developing quality measurement standards.

**HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)**
Federal law protecting patient health information privacy and security with compliance requirements.

**PHI (Protected Health Information)**
Individually identifiable health information subject to HIPAA privacy and security protections.

**ACA (Affordable Care Act)**
Federal healthcare reform law establishing coverage mandates, marketplaces, and consumer protections.

**Quality Measure**
Standardized metric evaluating healthcare process, outcome, or patient experience for improvement.

## Utilization Management

**Utilization Review**
Evaluation of medical service appropriateness, necessity, and efficiency before, during, or after care.

**Case Management**
Coordinated care approach for complex/high-cost members improving outcomes and reducing costs.

**Disease Management**
Program supporting members with chronic conditions through education, monitoring, and care coordination.

**Step Therapy**
Requirement to try lower-cost medications before payer covers higher-cost alternatives.

**Referral**
PCP authorization for member to see specialist or receive specific services required by some plans.

**Pre-Certification**
Verification of coverage and medical necessity before service delivery, similar to prior authorization.

## Pharmacy Benefits

**PBM (Pharmacy Benefit Manager)**
Third-party administrator managing prescription drug benefits, formularies, and pharmacy networks.

**NDC (National Drug Code)**
Unique identifier for drugs including manufacturer, product, and package size for billing.

**Tier**
Formulary level determining member cost-sharing (generic, preferred brand, non-preferred brand, specialty).

**Specialty Pharmacy**
Pharmacy managing high-cost, complex medications for chronic or rare conditions with special handling.

**Rebate**
Manufacturer payment to PBM/payer based on drug utilization negotiated in formulary placement.

**Days Supply**
Number of days prescription is intended to last, affecting quantity limits and refill timing.

## Data & Analytics

**Member Month**
Unit measuring coverage representing one member enrolled for one month, used for PMPM calculations.

**Enrollment**
Active membership count or process of member signing up for coverage during open enrollment.

**Attribution**
Assigning members to specific providers or risk entities for quality measurement and payment.

**Risk Score**
Numerical value predicting member's expected costs based on demographics and diagnoses (HCC model).

**Medical Cost**
Total spending on healthcare services including claims, pharmacy, and capitation payments.

**Administrative Cost**
Payer operational expenses for claims processing, customer service, and business operations.

**Trend**
Year-over-year percentage change in healthcare costs driven by utilization and unit cost increases.

**Population Health**
Data-driven approach managing health outcomes for defined member groups through prevention and care coordination.

## Common Payer Terms

**Accumulator**
Running total of member's deductible, out-of-pocket spending tracked during benefit period.

**Appeal**
Formal request to reconsider denied claim or coverage decision through internal or external review.

**Care Gap**
Evidence-based preventive or chronic care service member should receive but hasn't (HEDIS measures).

**Effective Date**
Date when member's coverage begins or change takes effect for eligibility and benefits.

**Termination Date**
Date when member's coverage ends due to non-payment, job loss, or voluntary cancellation.

**Grace Period**
Timeframe allowing premium payment after due date before coverage termination (typically 30-90 days).

**ID Card**
Physical or digital card showing member information, plan details, and numbers for provider billing.

**Open Enrollment**
Annual period when individuals can enroll, change, or cancel health insurance plans.

**Special Enrollment Period (SEP)**
Qualifying event allowing enrollment outside open enrollment (marriage, birth, job loss).

**Rx**
Abbreviation for prescription medication benefits and pharmacy claims.

**Preventive Care**
Services covered at 100% without cost-sharing including screenings, immunizations, and wellness visits (ACA requirement).

**Emergency Services**
Urgent medical care that must be covered regardless of provider network status under federal law.

**Grandfathered Plan**
Plan existing before ACA exempt from certain reform requirements if unchanged significantly.

**Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC)**
Standardized document explaining plan coverage, costs, and limitations in consumer-friendly format.
