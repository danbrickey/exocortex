## Program Overview

The Master of Science in Applied Artificial Intelligence (MSAAI) is designed for professionals seeking to apply AI technologies strategically across industries. The program focuses on practical AI integration, governance, and ethical implementation.

### Key Program Features
- **Total Credits**: 30 credit hours
- **Core Credits**: 27 credits (9 courses)
- **Elective Credits**: 3 credits (1 course)
- **Delivery Format**: Flexible online and in-person learning options
- **Target Audience**: Working professionals
- **Program Duration**: 5 semesters (part-time)

## Program Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of the MS in Applied AI, graduates will be able to:

1. **Assess business AI opportunities** - Identify and evaluate AI use cases across business functions
2. **Lead ethical AI product development** - Guide responsible AI implementation with ethical considerations
3. **Interpret machine learning models** - Understand ML model behavior, evaluation, and selection
4. **Develop AI data management strategies** - Design data architecture and governance for AI systems
5. **Understand AI regulatory implications** - Navigate legal, compliance, and regulatory requirements
6. **Implement inclusive AI design practices** - Build AI systems that address bias and promote fairness

## Graduation Requirements

- **Minimum GPA**: 3.0
- **Time Limit**: Complete all courses within 5 years
- **Transfer Credits**: Maximum 10 credits from other institutions

- **Capstone**: Successfully complete Applied AI Capstone Project (TECH 6910)

## Course Sequence (5 Semesters)

### Fall 2026 - Semester 1 (6 credits)
**Focus**: AI Foundations & Business Context

#### TECH 6100: Foundations of Applied Artificial Intelligence (3 credits)
- **Description**: Introduction to AI concepts, technologies, and applications
- **Topics**: AI history, core concepts, AI landscape, technology overview
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - Already doing: Andrew Ng ML Specialization
  - Read: General AI overview books (*The AI Playbook*, *Prediction Machines*)
  - Familiarize yourself with AI terminology and history

#### TECH 6200: Artificial Intelligence in Business (3 credits)
- **Description**: Strategic application of AI in business contexts
- **Topics**: AI use cases, business value, ROI frameworks, AI adoption strategies
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - Blue Cross AI Adoption Roadmap work (aligns perfectly)
  - Your AI Strategist skill-building activities
  - Read business-focused AI books
  - Document Blue Cross use cases and business value

---

### Spring 2027 - Semester 2 (6 credits)
**Focus**: Technical Skills & Ethics

#### INFO 6200: Python for Applied Artificial Intelligence (3 credits)
- **Description**: Python programming specifically for AI applications
- **Topics**: Python basics, data manipulation, AI libraries (NumPy, Pandas, scikit-learn)
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - Start learning Python basics (Q4 2025 - Q1 2026)
  - Online courses: Python for Data Science (Coursera, DataCamp)
  - Practice with Snowflake Python UDFs at Blue Cross
  - Work through Python ML tutorials

#### TECH 6250: Ethical Legal and Social Implications of Artificial Intelligence (3 credits)
- **Description**: Ethical considerations, legal frameworks, and social impact of AI
- **Topics**: AI ethics, bias, fairness, privacy, regulatory compliance, responsible AI
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - Study NIST AI Risk Management Framework
  - Read about EU AI Act, healthcare AI regulations
  - Follow AI ethics thought leaders (Timnit Gebru, Fei-Fei Li)
  - Apply HIPAA + AI considerations at Blue Cross

---

### Summer 2027 - Semester 3 (6 credits)
**Focus**: Data Strategy & Decision Making

#### TECH 6700: Data Driven Decision Making (3 credits)
- **Description**: Using data analytics and AI for strategic decision-making
- **Topics**: Analytics frameworks, data interpretation, business intelligence, decision science
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - You already have strong foundation from 25+ years in data
  - Document decision-making frameworks used at Blue Cross
  - Study AI-enhanced analytics approaches
  - Your EDP architecture work aligns well

#### TECH 6730: Data Strategy and Management for Artificial Intelligence (3 credits)
- **Description**: Data architecture, governance, and management for AI/ML systems
- **Topics**: Data quality, feature engineering, data pipelines, ML data requirements
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - This is your STRENGTH - Data Vault 2.0, medallion architecture, EDP
  - Document "AI-Ready Data Platform" architecture at Blue Cross
  - Study feature stores (Snowflake Feature Store, Feast, Tecton)
  - Your Solutions Architect work directly applies

---

### Fall 2027 - Semester 4 (6 credits)
**Focus**: Machine Learning & AI Implementation

#### TECH 6750: Strategic Implementation of AI Systems (3 credits)
- **Description**: Planning, deploying, and managing AI systems in production
- **Topics**: MLOps, AI system architecture, deployment patterns, monitoring
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - Study MLOps frameworks and tools
  - Read: *Designing Machine Learning Systems* by Chip Huyen
  - Snowflake Cortex AI deployment experience
  - Your Blue Cross AI implementation projects

#### CS 6470: Machine Learning (3 credits)
- **Description**: Core machine learning algorithms, model training, and evaluation
- **Topics**: Supervised/unsupervised learning, model selection, evaluation metrics, deep learning basics
- **Preparation Recommendations**:
  - Andrew Ng ML Specialization (completing before program starts)
  - Hands-on ML projects (Cortex AI POCs, portfolio projects)
  - Study model evaluation metrics, bias-variance tradeoff
  - Most technical course - ensure ML fundamentals are solid
